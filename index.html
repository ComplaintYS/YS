Why We Created This Website

I’ve been chartering yachts across Europe for decades, and I thought I’d seen it all—until I booked with yachtinginsardinia.com. From the moment we paid, the headaches started, even before we stepped on board: strange contract clauses, gear that didn’t work, boats that clearly weren’t ready, days stuck in port instead of out sailing, and even times when we genuinely didn’t feel safe.

The worst part? I didn’t see any of the bad reviews before booking. A lot of them don’t show up in Google results unless you dig. Once the trouble began, I started looking closer and found plenty of other sailors describing the same issues.

On disembarkation day it all came into focus—we weren’t alone. Crews were stuck on the dock, frustrated and ignored by the company. Most charter guests are international and don’t know how to navigate local consumer-protection rules, which is why these practices keep happening.

After the trip we kept talking to people in the marinas and met sailors who’d chartered with the same company during different weeks. They’d experienced the same problems — and had seen other crews dealing with them too. This isn’t random bad luck; it’s a pattern.

This website exists to:

  • Bring hidden reviews and real customer experiences into the light so you can book with your eyes open
  • Help sailors, skippers, and crews connect and compare notes
  • Document recurring safety and service problems
  • Push for more transparency in Sardinian yacht charters and help people seek refunds when things go wrong

If you’ve had a rough charter in Sardinia, you’re not the only one. Share your story, connect with others, and help us push for safer, fairer charters—for everyone who just wants to enjoy the wind and the sea.

Potentially Abusive Clauses in the Contract

Legal Disclaimer This information is not legal advice.

Key Issues Overview

These contract clauses may conflict with EU consumer protection laws and your rights as a customer.

No Refund Policy

Even if sailing is impossible – Conflicts with EU law requiring reimbursement for undelivered services

Exclusion of monetary refund

Even for breakdowns, only offering “time credit” for future charters – May conflict with consumer rights to price reduction.

Unilateral Departure Denial

Company can deny departure without refund based on subjective skill assessment

Excessive Penalties

Automatic credit card charges without proportionality or proper consent

Limited Liability

For yacht condition/breakdowns, despite EU service quality requirements

Jurisdiction Restriction

Italy-only jurisdiction violates EU Regulation 1215/2012 on consumer rights

Customer Liability

Clients bear all risks and damages regardless of fault
